Read the full narrative text (for reports and technical detail)

Hurricane: The risk rating for Baltimore, Montgomery, and Prince George’s Counties is relatively high for hurricanes. This is due to a combination of high population density and infrastructure exposure, high property values, and historical exposure to remnants of tropical storms and hurricanes.

Moderate risk areas include Harford, Baltimore City, Howard, Anne Arundel, Charles, St. Mary’s, Caroline, Talbot, Dorchester, Somerset, Wicomico, and Worcester. Although less urbanized than central counties, these areas face recurring exposure from strong tropical storms that can produce significant rainfall and moderate wind damage. These counties also tend to have a substantial number of people, homes, and businesses in areas exposed to hurricane impacts.

The relatively low and low risk areas experience weakened storm intensity as tropical systems lose strength over land. While remnants of hurricanes may bring heavy rain or isolated wind damage, the frequency and economic impact remain minimal compared to central and southern Maryland. Therefore, due to lower exposure, fewer structures, and weaker storm intensity by the time hurricanes reach these regions, they have relatively low to very low risk rating.
